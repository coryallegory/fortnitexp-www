#52 License Plates

  • I will buy one plate or an entire collection to get what I need, and I am always looking to purchase back my grandfather's #52 Manitoba license plates from 1911-1926. They were stolen from our family home while my father was fighting during WW2.

    No questions asked

    I don't care how, or who you acquired them from. Too much water has passed under the bridge in seventy-five years. My family and I would just like to complete the run of #52 plates from 1911 to 1926 as they were originally issued to my grandfather.

    Please Note: we are in possession of the original 1911 disc, and fellow collector Ed Marchuck was kind enough to trade back to us #52's from 1918 and 1919. That still leaves a number of #52's out there somewhere. I would appreciate your help.

Manitoba Motorcycle Plates (*I will pay more than anyone to complete my run.*)
  • 1919 - The 1919 Manitoba Motorcycle Plate is a small tab, approximately 1 3/4"(height) x 2"(width), that is white on green. This tab attaches to a 4"x6" 1918 base, and will have a 3-digit or less number on the bottom. They are like the 1919 car tags, only smaller.
  • 1922 - 1922 Manitoba Motorcycle plates measure 3" x 7.5", and are white on black. Same as car plate, only smaller.
  • 1927 - The 1927 Manitoba Motorcycle Plate is approximately 4.25"x6.25" and is black on off-white. Same as the car plate, only smaller.

Manitoba Pre-Issue License Aluminum Seals

  • From 1908 until 1910, cars were licensed by issuing a registration paper, and a round aluminum seal with numbers that would correspond to the car owner's homemade leather plates. The round seal was replaced in 1911 with an oval seal, which in turn was replaced halfway through the year with Manitoba's first porcelain plates in 1911.

    Here's an etching of one of these discs. It's diameter is 2".
